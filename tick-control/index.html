Understanding the Lifecycle and Habits of Ticks

Effective tick control begins with a deep understanding of these fascinating, albeit problematic, creatures. Ticks in Milford city, CT go through several life stages: egg, larva, nymph, and adult. Each stage has its own characteristics and preferences. Larval and nymphal ticks are particularly concerning because they are smaller and harder to detect, yet they are often the transmitters of diseases like Lyme disease. Adult ticks are larger but also pose a threat. Ticks don't fly or jump; they 'quest' by climbing onto vegetation and waiting for a host to brush by. They are commonly found in wooded areas, tall grass, leaf litter, and around the perimeter of properties, especially where lawns transition into natural areas. Understanding these habits allows us to target our treatments precisely where ticks are most likely to be found and where they pose the greatest risk.

Understanding the Different Types of Ticks in Milford city

While the blacklegged tick (also known as the deer tick) is the primary concern for Lyme disease transmission in Milford city, CT, other tick species can also be found in the area and pose different risks. The American dog tick, for example, is larger and more commonly encountered in grassy and brushy areas, and can transmit Rocky Mountain spotted fever and tularemia. The lone star tick, identifiable by the white spot on the female's back, is also present in Connecticut and can cause Southern tick-associated rash illness (STARI) and allergic reactions to red meat (Alpha-gal syndrome). While the risks vary between species, the general principles of tick prevention and control remain important. Our experienced technicians are trained to identify the different tick species present on your property and implement control measures that are effective against the specific ticks posing a threat.
